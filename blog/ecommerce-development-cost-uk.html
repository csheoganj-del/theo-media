How Much Does Ecommerce Website Development Cost in the UK? (2026)

By TheoMedia Editorial Team 27 May 2026 10 min read Web Development

Building an ecommerce website is one of the most significant digital investments a UK business makes. The price range is enormous — from £25/month for a Shopify Basic subscription to £500,000+ for an enterprise custom platform — and somewhere in between is the right answer for your business. The wrong choice in either direction has real consequences: underspend and you'll hit a ceiling within two years; overspend and you'll struggle to justify the ROI.

This guide gives you an honest, complete breakdown of ecommerce development costs in the UK for 2026. We cover every major approach — SaaS platforms like Shopify, open-source platforms like WooCommerce, and fully custom-built ecommerce systems — with realistic numbers, a clear view of hidden costs, and a framework for choosing the right approach based on your revenue stage and requirements.

Ecommerce Platform Options: The Three Approaches

Approach 1: SaaS Ecommerce Platforms (Shopify, BigCommerce, Wix eCommerce)

SaaS (Software as a Service) ecommerce platforms host everything for you. You pay a monthly subscription and get access to hosting, security, payment processing infrastructure, and a managed admin interface. The most prominent in the UK market is Shopify, followed by BigCommerce.

Best for: Businesses launching quickly, product-focused stores without unusual requirements, businesses without in-house technical expertise.

Limitations: Monthly fees accumulate significantly over time; transaction fees eat into margins; customisation beyond the platform's design system requires expensive developer work; platform lock-in makes migration costly later.

Approach 2: Open-Source Platform (WooCommerce on WordPress)

WooCommerce is a free ecommerce plugin for WordPress, the world's most widely used CMS. You host it yourself, own the codebase, and extend it with plugins. The WooCommerce core is free; hosting, premium extensions, and development time are not.

Best for: Businesses wanting content marketing integration, businesses with reasonable technical resources, stores needing custom product types or unusual workflows that Shopify can't accommodate.

Limitations: Requires ongoing maintenance (WordPress and plugin updates), more vulnerable to security issues if not properly managed, can become slow and unstable with too many plugins.

Approach 3: Custom Ecommerce Development

A fully custom ecommerce platform built specifically for your business, using modern frameworks (Next.js, Laravel, Node.js, PostgreSQL). No monthly platform fees, no plugin dependencies, no constraints on functionality. Built exactly to your requirements and owned entirely by you.

Best for: Businesses with unusual product types or pricing models, businesses needing deep integration with ERP/stock/accounting systems, businesses processing significant volume where platform fees become expensive, or businesses with highly specific UX requirements.

Limitations: Higher upfront investment, requires ongoing developer relationship for maintenance and new features.

Hidden Costs of Shopify and WooCommerce

Shopify Hidden Costs

Shopify's headline pricing is deceptive. Here's what you'll typically spend beyond the base subscription:

  • Transaction fees: If you use any payment gateway other than Shopify Payments, you pay 0.5–2% on every transaction. On £30,000/month in revenue, that's £150–£600 per month in fees alone.
  • Premium theme: A decent Shopify theme costs £100–£350 one-time. Without design work, all stores using the same theme look identical.
  • Apps: The Shopify App Store has thousands of apps, many of which charge monthly subscriptions. A typical growing store uses 8–12 apps, spending £80–£400/month on subscriptions for features that would be built-in on a custom platform.
  • Custom development: Any customisation beyond what a theme supports requires a Shopify developer. Shopify's Liquid templating language is a proprietary skill — not all developers know it, which limits your hiring options and increases costs.

WooCommerce Hidden Costs

  • Managed WordPress hosting: WooCommerce performs best on managed WordPress hosting (WP Engine, Kinsta, Pressable) — not cheap shared hosting. Expect £30–£80/month for a store with reasonable traffic.
  • Premium plugins: Essential WooCommerce extensions (advanced product variations, subscriptions, bookings, memberships, advanced shipping) carry annual licence fees of £50–£200 each.
  • Maintenance time: WordPress and WooCommerce require regular updates. Neglected updates lead to security vulnerabilities. Either spend time managing updates yourself or pay £50–£150/month for a maintenance service.
  • Performance issues: WooCommerce on shared hosting with many plugins is slow. Performance optimisation work — caching, CDN setup, image compression, database cleanup — is an ongoing cost.

Custom Ecommerce: When It Makes Financial Sense

A custom ecommerce build becomes financially rational when:

  • Your annual Shopify or WooCommerce costs (platform + apps + developer time) exceed £5,000/year — at which point a custom build amortises within 2–3 years.
  • You're processing over £250,000/year in online revenue — at which point transaction fees on Shopify (if not using Shopify Payments) represent a significant annual cost.
  • Your product catalogue has unusual requirements — configurable products, custom pricing rules, subscription models — that platform plugins handle clumsily.
  • You need deep integration with your stock management, accounting, or ERP system that would require expensive custom middleware on a SaaS platform.
  • You're selling in multiple currencies and need precise control over exchange rates, pricing by market, and country-specific tax rules.

Multi-Currency Ecommerce for UK/EU Businesses

Post-Brexit, many UK businesses sell into both the UK (GBP) and EU (EUR) markets. Multi-currency ecommerce requires more than just displaying prices in different currencies — it requires currency-appropriate payment processing, correct VAT handling per jurisdiction, and ideally separate pricing strategies per market.

On Shopify, multi-currency is supported via Shopify Markets — but the VAT handling requires careful configuration and may not satisfy complex B2B scenarios. On custom builds, TheoMedia implements full multi-currency support including currency-based pricing tables, Stripe's multi-currency charge capabilities, and EU VAT OSS compliance where required.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to build an ecommerce website in the UK?

The cheapest way to launch is Shopify Basic at £25/month, or WooCommerce on inexpensive shared hosting. However, cheap upfront often means expensive over time — transaction fees, app subscriptions, and developer costs accumulate. For long-term value, consider total cost of ownership across 3 years rather than monthly cost alone.

How long does it take to build a custom ecommerce website?

A custom ecommerce build typically takes 6–14 weeks from kickoff to launch. A store with 50–200 products and standard checkout flows takes around 6–8 weeks. A multi-currency store with custom product configurators and third-party integrations may take 12–16 weeks depending on scope and complexity.

Is Shopify or WooCommerce better for a UK ecommerce business?

Both have strengths. Shopify is easier to manage and ideal for product-focused stores that want a fast launch. WooCommerce offers more flexibility and no platform transaction fees — better for stores with unusual product types or tight system integrations. For stores generating significant revenue, a custom build often provides the best value over a 3-year horizon.

Does a UK ecommerce site need to handle VAT automatically?

Yes. UK VAT must be applied correctly at checkout for sales to UK consumers. For EU sales post-Brexit, the OSS (One Stop Shop) VAT rules apply for B2C transactions. Ensure your ecommerce platform can handle both UK VAT registration and EU VAT OSS remittance, with correct VAT display and reporting for your accountant or tax software.
