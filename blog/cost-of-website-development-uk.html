How Much Does a Website Cost in the UK? (2026 Pricing Guide)

By TheoMedia Editorial Team 14 June 2026 10 min read Web Development

Type "how much does a website cost UK" into Google and you'll find answers ranging from "completely free" to "£100,000+". Both are technically correct, which makes the question almost useless without context. The reality is that website development cost in the UK is driven by a handful of well-defined factors — and once you understand those factors, you can set a realistic budget and avoid overpaying or underbuying.

This guide breaks down every cost tier in the UK web development market for 2026: from DIY website builders to bespoke agency projects, with honest figures, hidden costs most people overlook, and a clear explanation of what separates a £950 website from a £15,000 one. Whether you're a small business owner getting your first site, or a growing company considering a complete rebuild, this guide will help you spend your money wisely.

The Main Factors That Affect Website Cost

Before we get to numbers, it's important to understand what actually drives the price of a website. These are the primary variables:

  • Number of pages: A 5-page brochure site is very different from a 200-page e-commerce store. More pages mean more content, more design work, and more development time.
  • Custom design vs template: A website built on a pre-made theme (Squarespace, Wix, or a WordPress theme) costs far less than one designed from scratch in Figma and coded by hand. Custom design means your site is unique, reflects your brand precisely, and is built exactly to your needs.
  • Functionality and integrations: Does your site need a booking system? A customer portal? Payment processing? Third-party API integrations (Stripe, Xero, Mailchimp)? Every piece of custom functionality adds development time.
  • Who builds it: A junior freelancer, a senior freelancer, a boutique agency, a large UK agency, or an offshore agency each charge very different rates. We'll compare these in detail below.
  • Content: Some agencies include copywriting and photography sourcing; others expect you to provide it. This can swing the total cost by £500–£3,000.
  • Ongoing requirements: Will you need monthly updates, SEO work, hosting management, or security monitoring? These are recurring costs that must be factored into your total budget.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Even after your website is built, the costs don't stop. Many business owners are surprised by the ongoing expenses that weren't clearly communicated at the start. Here are the ones to budget for:

Domain Registration

A .co.uk domain typically costs £10–£15 per year. A .com costs £12–£25. Premium or brandable domains can cost hundreds or thousands. Domain registration is separate from your website build cost — always clarify this with your developer.

Web Hosting

Hosting costs vary enormously depending on what type of site you have. A static or simple brochure site can be hosted free on Vercel or Netlify, or for £5–£20/month on shared hosting. A WordPress site needs managed WordPress hosting at £15–£60/month for decent performance. A custom web application may require a VPS or cloud server at £30–£150/month depending on traffic.

SSL Certificate

SSL (the padlock in your browser) is free with Let's Encrypt and included with most modern hosting providers. However, if your developer hasn't set this up, a commercial SSL certificate costs £40–£200/year. Always confirm SSL is included.

Maintenance and Updates

WordPress sites require regular plugin updates, security patches, and database maintenance. Neglecting this leads to security breaches. Expect to pay £50–£200/month for professional maintenance, or allow a full day per month if doing it yourself. Custom-coded sites require far less maintenance — typically just quarterly check-ups.

Email Hosting

Professional email (yourname@yourbusiness.co.uk) is separate from website hosting. Google Workspace costs £4.99–£13.50 per user per month. Microsoft 365 is comparable. Budget around £60–£100/year per email address.

How to Budget for Your Website in 2026

Here's a practical framework for setting your website budget, regardless of what stage your business is at:

Stage 1: New Business (Under 2 Years)

Budget £950–£2,500 for a professionally designed, bespoke brochure site. Avoid DIY builders if you're serious about SEO and conversion — the performance penalty alone will cost you more in lost leads than the savings on the monthly fee. Focus on: clear messaging, a working contact form, and fast load times.

Stage 2: Growing Business (2–5 Years)

Budget £2,000–£6,000 for a more feature-rich site with booking systems, case study/portfolio sections, a blog, and integration with your CRM or email marketing platform. This is where a custom web development partner becomes essential.

Stage 3: Established Business (5+ Years)

Budget £5,000–£20,000 for a strategic rebuild. At this stage, your website should be a business system, not just a brochure. Consider integrating it with your stock management, order processing, or customer management workflows. A custom web application may make more sense than a traditional website.

Rule of thumb: Your website should cost roughly 5–10% of your annual revenue target from digital channels. If you expect to generate £50,000/year through your website, a £2,500–£5,000 investment is entirely reasonable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a basic business website cost in the UK?

A basic 5–8 page business website from a professional agency typically costs between £950 and £3,000 in the UK. DIY builders like Wix or Squarespace start from around £12–£35 per month but come with design limitations and ongoing fees that add up significantly over time.

Why is there such a big price range for UK web development?

The range exists because "a website" can mean anything from a one-page landing page to a custom ecommerce platform with 10,000 products and a bespoke CRM integration. The main cost drivers are complexity, number of pages, custom functionality, and whether you're using templates or bespoke design and code.

Are India-based web agencies reliable for UK businesses?

Yes — many India-based agencies deliver excellent quality at significantly lower cost. The key is choosing an agency with proven UK client experience, strong communication standards, GDPR knowledge, and transparent project management. TheoMedia, for example, operates with a UK phone number (+353 85 225 8004) and specifically serves the UK and European market.

What ongoing costs should I budget for after my website is built?

Expect to budget for domain renewal (£10–£25/year), web hosting (£5–£80/month depending on site size), and ongoing maintenance. A maintenance retainer from a professional agency typically costs £50–£300/month and covers security patches, performance monitoring, and content updates.

Is a template website or a bespoke website better value?

Templates are cheaper upfront but often cost more over three years when you factor in monthly platform fees, plugin costs, and the inability to scale without expensive workarounds. Bespoke websites require a larger initial investment but are faster, more secure, fully owned by you, and built exactly to your business requirements — which typically means better SEO and conversion rates.
