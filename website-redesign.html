Six Signs Your Website Needs a Redesign

These are the clearest indicators that your current website is holding your business back rather than supporting its growth.

🐢

Slow Load Times

Your site takes more than three seconds to load on mobile. Google penalises slow sites in search rankings, and visitors abandon them before they load.

📱

Poor Mobile Experience

Text is too small to read, buttons are too close together to tap, or the layout breaks on smartphones. Over 60% of UK web traffic is mobile.

🎨

Outdated Visual Design

Your website looks like it was built five years ago — because it was. A dated design erodes trust, particularly for professional services and premium brands.

📉

Poor Search Rankings

Your Core Web Vitals are failing, your on-page SEO is unstructured, and competitors with better-built sites are ranking above you for your target keywords.

💬

High Bounce Rate

Visitors arrive on your site and leave immediately — without clicking, enquiring, or converting. Your analytics show bounce rates above 70% for key landing pages.

🔧

Difficult to Update

Making simple content changes requires a developer, or your WordPress installation is a tangle of plugins that break on every update. Maintaining the site costs more than it should.

The TheoMedia Redesign Approach

A website redesign is not simply a cosmetic refresh — it's a strategic exercise that should simultaneously improve your design, your performance, your SEO, and your conversion rate. Done properly, a redesign delivers measurable improvements across every metric that matters: organic search rankings, page load speed, mobile usability score, conversion rate, and bounce rate. Done carelessly, a redesign can wipe out years of accumulated SEO equity in a single launch.

Our redesign process is structured to capture every opportunity for improvement while protecting everything you've earned. We begin with a comprehensive audit of your existing site — not just the design, but the technical architecture, the SEO equity, the content quality, and the user journey. That audit informs every subsequent decision: which pages to keep exactly as they are, which to improve, which to consolidate, and which to remove.

Preserving Your SEO During a Redesign

The most common redesign mistake is changing URL structures without implementing proper 301 redirects — resulting in 404 errors for pages that have accumulated inbound links and search engine authority over years. We handle this with rigorous process. Before any URL is changed, we document every existing URL and its Google Search Console performance data. For every URL that changes, we implement a 301 permanent redirect from the old address to the new one — preserving the link equity and signalling to Google that the content has moved, not disappeared. We also preserve and improve existing meta titles, meta descriptions, heading structures, and structured data during the redesign, rather than resetting them from scratch.

HOW WE WORK

Our Website Redesign Process

A five-stage process that transforms your underperforming website into a high-converting digital asset — safely, without risking your SEO.

01

Site Audit & SEO Inventory

We crawl your existing site, documenting every URL, its traffic volume, backlink profile, meta data, and current rankings. This becomes our migration map — ensuring nothing of SEO value is lost during the transition to the new site.

02

Strategy & Information Architecture

Based on the audit, we recommend a new site structure: pages to keep, pages to improve, pages to consolidate, and new pages to create. We produce a revised sitemap that reflects your current business — not the business you were when the site was first built.

03

Design & Content Migration Plan

Visual design in Figma, reviewed and approved before development begins. Simultaneously, we identify which existing content is worth keeping, which needs rewriting, and which should be replaced — creating a content migration plan with your team.

04

Development & Redirect Mapping

Clean code development — HTML/CSS/JS or React/Next.js — with full implementation of the 301 redirect map, canonical URL configuration, structured data, and performance optimisation. The new site is built on a staging environment and tested thoroughly before launch.

05

Launch, Monitoring & Post-Launch Support

Launch at low-traffic time, immediate redirect verification, Google Search Console resubmission, and active monitoring of rankings and crawl errors for 30 days post-launch to catch and resolve any indexing issues promptly.

Your rankings can be significantly harmed by a redesign if it's handled carelessly — or they can be improved by a redesign handled correctly. The primary risks are: changing URLs without 301 redirects (causing 404 errors and losing link equity), removing SEO-critical content, resetting meta tags incorrectly, and degrading page speed by migrating to a heavier platform. We address all of these systematically: every URL is mapped before the redesign, every change is accompanied by a 301 redirect, meta data is preserved and improved, and performance is benchmarked before and after. Many of our redesign clients see ranking improvements within sixty to ninety days of launch because the new site's technical quality — faster load times, better mobile usability, cleaner code — is rewarded by Google's algorithms.

A standard redesign of a five-to-fifteen page website — including audit, strategy, design, development, content migration, and launch — typically takes six to ten weeks. Larger sites with thirty or more pages, ecommerce functionality, or complex custom integrations take twelve to sixteen weeks. The audit and strategy phase is non-negotiable — rushing it creates the exact kinds of SEO problems we're trying to avoid. We always provide a firm timeline in your proposal, and we build buffer time into every phase to accommodate content review rounds and client feedback without impacting the overall launch date.
