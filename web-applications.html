The Problem With Off-the-Shelf Software

Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics, QuickBooks, Sage — these platforms are built to serve hundreds of thousands of businesses across dozens of industries. That breadth is precisely their weakness. To accommodate every possible use case, they become enormously complex: dense with features you'll never use, constrained in the ways that matter most to you, and expensive at a scale that grows with your headcount rather than your actual needs.

UK businesses in hospitality, retail, manufacturing, and import/export frequently find themselves spending significant money on platform licences, then spending additional money on implementation consultants, then spending more money on custom plugins or workarounds — only to end up with a system that still doesn't quite fit how their business actually operates. The cumulative cost of compromising with off-the-shelf software, year after year, frequently exceeds the cost of a bespoke solution.

TheoMedia builds web applications that do exactly what your business requires — nothing more, nothing less. Because the system is designed around your processes rather than a generic template, adoption rates are higher, training requirements are lower, and the ROI is measurable from day one.

Web Application FAQs

What UK businesses want to know before replacing their off-the-shelf software with a bespoke web application.

Salesforce and HubSpot are powerful platforms — if your business fits neatly into their standard model. For many UK businesses, particularly in B2B services, hospitality, or niche professional sectors, they don't. A custom CRM built by TheoMedia is designed around your actual sales process, your terminology, and your reporting needs. There are no unused modules cluttering your interface. Your team doesn't need to adapt their workflows to the software — the software is built around your workflows. Additionally, you pay once for development rather than recurring per-seat licences that scale with headcount and become expensive as your team grows. For a team of 20 people paying £100/month per seat for Salesforce, you're spending £24,000 per year — often more than the cost of a bespoke system that you own outright.

We integrate your web application with the WhatsApp Business API — the official, programmatic access to WhatsApp messaging for businesses. When your staff marks an invoice as ready to send, the system generates a professional PDF invoice and dispatches it directly to your client's WhatsApp number, along with a personalised message and a Stripe payment link. The client receives the invoice in their WhatsApp — no login required, no email to search for — and can pay instantly via card. Your system receives a payment confirmation webhook and automatically marks the invoice as paid. Read receipts confirm the client has seen the message. For businesses dealing with clients in markets where WhatsApp is the dominant communication platform, this reduces average payment time from 30+ days to often same-day or next-day settlement.

Yes — we can build offline capability into web applications using Progressive Web App (PWA) technology and local caching strategies. For POS systems in particular, offline mode is often critical: your checkout terminal needs to keep working even if the Wi-Fi drops. We implement local data caching so transactions are recorded offline and automatically synced to the central database when connectivity is restored. This is more complex to build than a purely online application, and we'll discuss your specific offline requirements during the discovery phase to ensure the architecture is appropriate for your environment.

Security is built into our architecture from the ground up. All data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). We deploy on AWS infrastructure with VPC isolation, security group rules, and automated intrusion detection. Database access is restricted by role and IP whitelist. All user authentication uses bcrypt password hashing with optional two-factor authentication. We implement audit logging so every data access and modification is recorded with timestamps and user IDs — essential for regulated industries and GDPR compliance. We never store sensitive payment card data — Stripe handles all card processing in a fully PCI-DSS compliant environment.
