Custom Ecommerce vs Shopify & WooCommerce

Shopify and WooCommerce have made ecommerce accessible to millions of businesses, and for many small retailers that's absolutely sufficient. But there are significant limitations that become increasingly painful as your business grows. Shopify charges transaction fees on every sale (0.5%–2% depending on your plan) unless you use Shopify Payments — which isn't always available or suitable for UK businesses. WooCommerce on WordPress requires constant plugin updates, plugin conflicts cause unexplained breakages, and the performance overhead of a WordPress installation creates slow load times that directly harm conversion rates.

For UK retailers with more than a few hundred products, international aspirations (selling in GBP, EUR, and CHF), or specific checkout requirements (VAT-inclusive pricing, country-based shipping rules, B2B net terms), a custom-built ecommerce platform is a significantly better long-term investment. You pay once for development, you own the codebase outright, and there are no percentage fees on your revenue.

TheoMedia builds custom ecommerce websites using React and Next.js on the frontend with a Node.js API backend and PostgreSQL database. Payments are handled by Stripe — the UK's leading payment processor, with full support for 3D Secure, Apple Pay, Google Pay, BACS direct debit, and all major card networks. The result is a shop that's fast, secure, scalable, and entirely under your control.

Performance: The Ecommerce Conversion Factor

Google's research consistently demonstrates that a one-second improvement in mobile page load time increases ecommerce conversion rates by up to 27%. Every 100ms delay costs conversion. Our custom ecommerce builds target sub-2-second page loads on mobile networks, Lighthouse performance scores of 95+, and perfect Core Web Vitals — not because we want impressive numbers, but because page speed directly translates to revenue. Shopify themes and WooCommerce installations routinely load in 4–6 seconds on mobile. That gap is costing UK retailers sales every single day.

We use Stripe's official APIs and SDKs to build a fully integrated payment flow. When a customer reaches checkout, payment details are collected securely via Stripe Elements (hosted, PCI-compliant card input fields). We handle the full payment intent lifecycle — card authorisation, 3D Secure authentication where required, payment capture, and webhook-based order confirmation. Stripe handles all sensitive card data — it never touches our servers — so you inherit Stripe's PCI-DSS Level 1 compliance. We configure Apple Pay and Google Pay for one-tap mobile checkout, which typically increases mobile conversion rates significantly. Refunds, disputes, and payouts are all manageable via your Stripe dashboard, which we set up under your account so you have full control and visibility over your revenue.

VAT handling is one of the more complex aspects of ecommerce for UK businesses selling to European customers post-Brexit. We implement destination-based VAT calculation: prices displayed to UK customers include UK VAT (20%), prices displayed to EU customers include the appropriate EU member state VAT rate, and prices displayed to non-VAT registered business buyers in B2B scenarios can be shown ex-VAT. For UK businesses registered for the EU's One Stop Shop (OSS) scheme, we build reporting that generates OSS quarterly return data. VAT-compliant invoices are generated automatically with each order. We'll discuss your specific VAT obligations during the discovery phase to ensure the implementation is correct for your registration status and markets.
